ITIII Blog Entries

Outputs/Learnings

  • Professions & Careers in Computing

    The "Professions & Careers in Computing" lesson showed me how big and exciting the world of computers is. It made me realize that technology is everywhere and that there are so many different jobs related to it. From creating cool apps to protecting important information, there's something for everyone. The lesson taught me that learning new things and staying up-to-date is key to success in this field. It inspired me to think about how I can use technology to make a difference.

  • Information Processing Cycle

    In this lesson, I learned the difference between raw data and useful information. I understood how data, like numbers or facts, becomes information when it's organized and makes sense. I also learned about the process of turning data into information, which involves taking in data, processing it, and then getting useful information out. Lastly, I discovered the qualities of good information, such as being accurate, relevant, timely, complete, and unbiased.

  • Introduction to Computer System

    I learned about the four main parts of a computer system. Hardware is the physical stuff like the keyboard and monitor. Software is the invisible programs that tell the hardware what to do. Peopleware refers to the people who use and maintain the computer, like us. Lastly, dataware is the information and data that the computer processes. Understanding these parts helps us appreciate how computers work and how we can use them effectively.

Highlight

The "Professions & Careers in Computing" lesson was a real eye-opener for me. It showed me all the cool jobs I could have in the IT world. As a first-year IT student, it helped me think about what I really want to do in the future. It was exciting to learn about so many different career paths and it made me want to work hard to achieve my goals.
